### Historical UK Divorce Rates: Trends Over Time

The **divorce rate UK** has changed significantly over the decades:

**1970s-1980s:** Divorce rates rose sharply following the Divorce Reform Act 1969, which introduced "irretrievable breakdown" as grounds for divorce.

**1990s-2000s:** Rates peaked in the early 2000s with over 150,000 divorces annually.

**2010-2020:** A gradual decline occurred, with contributing factors including:
- Changing attitudes towards marriage
- Increased cohabitation without marriage
- Rising age at first marriage
- Economic factors

**2022-Present:** The introduction of no-fault divorce in April 2022 caused an initial spike in applications, now stabilising.

### Factors Affecting UK Divorce Rates

Several factors influence the **UK divorce rate**:

**Legal Changes**
The no-fault divorce law (April 2022) removed the need to assign blame, making the process less adversarial. This initially increased divorce applications as couples who had been waiting could proceed more easily.

**Economic Factors**
- Cost of living pressures can strain marriages
- Housing costs may delay or prevent separation
- Financial independence for women has increased divorce rates historically

**Social Changes**
- Reduced stigma around divorce
- Changing expectations of marriage
- Greater focus on personal fulfilment

**Age at Marriage**
Later marriages tend to be more stable. The rising average age at first marriage correlates with declining divorce rates.
