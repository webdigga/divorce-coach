## Understanding Divorce Coach Earnings in the UK

One of the most common questions from aspiring divorce coaches is about earning potential. The answer varies significantly depending on your commitment, experience, and business model. Here's a realistic look at what you can expect.

### Typical Session Rates

Divorce coaches in the UK typically charge between **£75 and £200 per session**, with sessions usually lasting 60-90 minutes. Several factors influence where you fall on this spectrum:

**Entry-Level Coaches (£75-£100 per session)**
- Recently certified
- Building their client base
- Limited specialisation
- Often working part-time

**Established Coaches (£100-£150 per session)**
- 1-3 years of experience
- Growing reputation and referrals
- May have developed a niche
- Consistent client flow

**Experienced Specialists (£150-£200+ per session)**
- 3+ years of experience
- Strong reputation and referral network
- Specialised expertise (e.g., high-net-worth divorces, narcissistic abuse)
- May offer premium packages

### Income Scenarios

Let's look at realistic income scenarios based on different commitment levels:

#### Side Hustle Model
**5-8 clients per month**

At £100 per session average:
- Monthly income: **£500 - £800**
- Annual income: **£6,000 - £9,600**

Perfect for those wanting to supplement existing income while helping others.

#### Part-Time Practice
**15-20 clients per month**

At £100 per session average:
- Monthly income: **£1,500 - £2,000**
- Annual income: **£18,000 - £24,000**

Ideal for those balancing coaching with other commitments.

#### Full-Time Practice
**30-40 clients per month**

At £120 per session average:
- Monthly income: **£3,600 - £4,800**
- Annual income: **£43,200 - £57,600**

Represents a sustainable full-time income for committed coaches.

#### Premium Practice
**30-40 clients per month**

At £175 per session average:
- Monthly income: **£5,250 - £7,000**
- Annual income: **£63,000 - £84,000**

Achievable for experienced coaches with strong reputations and specialisations.

### Additional Revenue Streams

Successful divorce coaches often diversify their income through:

**Group Coaching**
- Lower per-person rate but higher total income per hour
- Typical rate: £30-£50 per person per session
- Groups of 6-10 participants

**Workshops and Courses**
- Online courses: £97-£497 one-time fee
- In-person workshops: £50-£150 per attendee
- Passive income potential from recorded content

**Corporate Programmes**
- Employee wellbeing packages: £1,000-£5,000+ per contract
- Growing demand from employers recognising the impact of divorce on productivity

**Books and Digital Products**
- E-books, workbooks, and resources
- Establishes authority while generating passive income

### Factors That Increase Your Earning Potential

**1. Specialisation**
Coaches who specialise in specific areas can command higher rates:
- High-net-worth divorces
- Narcissistic abuse recovery
- Co-parenting coaching
- Corporate programmes

**2. Geographic Location**
London and Southeast coaches often charge 20-30% more than those in other regions, though online coaching is reducing this gap.

**3. Marketing and Visibility**
Coaches who invest in their online presence, content marketing, and networking typically build fuller practices faster.

**4. Continued Professional Development**
Additional certifications and training allow you to serve more complex cases and justify higher rates.

**5. Client Results and Testimonials**
Strong results lead to referrals—the most valuable source of new clients.

### The Investment vs. Return

Divorce coach training typically costs £1,000-£5,000. Let's put this in perspective:

At an average rate of £100 per session:
- 10-50 sessions to recoup your training investment
- Most coaches achieve this within their first 6-12 months
- Everything after that is profit (minus ongoing business costs)

### Is It Worth It?

The financial potential of divorce coaching is real, but it's important to be realistic:

**Pros:**
- Flexible schedule and location
- Growing demand for services
- Multiple revenue stream opportunities
- Low startup costs compared to many businesses
- Deeply meaningful work

**Cons:**
- Takes time to build a client base
- Income can be variable, especially initially
- Requires ongoing marketing and business development
- Emotional labour involved
