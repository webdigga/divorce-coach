Training costs vary based on accreditation and depth. Shorter online courses can start from £500-£3,000. More comprehensive, professionally accredited programmes, especially those aligned with recognised bodies, often cost around £3,000-£7,000+.

Some introductory or certificate programmes can be low cost (e.g., under £200) but may not be accredited by major bodies. ICF-aligned or professional relationship coach training typically ranges from a few hundred to several thousand pounds/dollars depending on length and depth.

Accredited programmes in divorce and breakup coaching usually cost in the mid-range, often roughly £1,000-£5,000+ depending on provider and support included. Higher-level specialist programmes with mentoring and extended business training may cost more.

Not all coaching courses are accredited, so check carefully. Some professional programmes are accredited by recognised bodies such as the International Coaching Federation (ICF) or other industry bodies - which can enhance credibility and client trust.

Accredited courses often:

  • Meet industry standards
  • Support eligibility for later credentialing (e.g., ACC, CPD, PCC with ICF)
  • Include ethical frameworks and consistent quality training

Strong programmes include:

  • Core coaching models and theory
  • Practical coaching skills and live/peer practice
  • Application to real client scenarios
  • Business and ethical best practice

Look for courses that combine theory + practicum + supervised practice - these prepare you better for real coaching situations.

Quality training programmes typically offer:

  • Live trainer mentoring and supervision sessions
  • Peer practice groups
  • Feedback on coaching practice
  • Business development guidance
  • Continued community or alumni support

Some accredited programmes include ongoing webinars, community groups, or mentoring after training.

No prior coaching experience is strictly required. Many programmes accept beginners as long as you are motivated to learn and practice coaching skills. However, life experience, strong interpersonal skills, and a growth mindset are helpful - especially for niches like divorce coaching where empathy and communication are vital.
